Chart pills update after merge: High (WDI) when the live indicator loaded; Partial WDI if the request failed for that series. If you open this file as file://, the browser may block the API—use a local HTTP server (e.g. VS Code Live Server) so WDI can load. Illegal migration and composite “indices” here are not official cross-country statistics.

history GDP & income per capita (WDI)

Documented macro shocks and expansions (IMF, World Bank, central banks) help explain synchronized dips and surges in output and GDP per capita—not precise year-by-year causality for every country.

Often supportive / expansionary

  • 1990s–2000s globalization & IT boom — trade integration and productivity gains in many economies (uneven).
  • Post–Cold War opening (early 1990s) — market access for several former planned economies.
  • 2010s recovery — rebound after the GFC where policy support restored demand (timing varied).

Often contractionary / volatile

  • 1973 & 1979 oil shocks — recessions and stagflation in many oil importers.
  • 1997–1998 Asian crisis — sharp currency and output collapses in several Asian economies.
  • 1998 Russia default — deep contraction and ruble stress.
  • 2008–2009 GFC — synchronized trade and GDP falls after the US credit shock.
  • 2020 COVID-19 — abrupt output drops from lockdowns and supply-chain breaks.
  • 2022+ energy & inflation shock — Ukraine war, sanctions, and commodity prices hit real incomes.
